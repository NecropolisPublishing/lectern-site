About Necropolis Publishing

The Legend

Throughout history, authors have gathered in speakeasies and back-room bars — places that came to be known as Haunts, where a writer could speak the unspeakable. But when ghostwriters gather, the unspeakable is spoken in a stranger faith: that everyone present already knows better than to repeat it. A ghostwriter, after all, is only as good as their silence — and politicians and pop stars alike shudder at the thought of one giving away the truths they were paid to bury.

In time, one haunt of ghostwriters decided to break the spell — to spill the beans, disguised as fiction. That haunt was known as Necropolis: the largest gathering of the most wretched-minded ghostwriters the world has never seen.

Today, Necropolis Publishing — hidden in the hills of north Georgia — is the fortress for those who write from the shadows and stir the cauldron of a quieter, hidden society.

Lectern is the first tool to leave the workshop: a quiet room built by people who write for a living, for anyone with a story to finish.
